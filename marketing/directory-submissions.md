### Long Description: Free Tool Directories

ApplyReadyCV is a free, privacy-first resume and CV checker for job seekers. It reviews common ATS readability, resume keyword, achievement, completeness, and job-fit issues for remote, freelance, and local applications. Users can paste resume text or upload supported files, optionally add a job description, and get practical editing guidance without an account. Resume text is analyzed locally in the browser session. ApplyReadyCV is informational only and does not guarantee interviews, job offers, recruiter decisions, or ATS approval.

### Long Description: Startup Directories

ApplyReadyCV helps job seekers review resumes and CVs before applying. The free browser-based checker gives practical feedback on ATS readability, missing keywords, achievements, completeness, and application fit. It supports remote, freelance, and local job use cases, and includes educational guide pages around ATS formatting, resume keywords, remote CVs, freelance profiles, local applications, and common CV mistakes. The product is privacy-first: no account, resume database, ads, payments, or third-party resume API in the current MVP.

### Long Description: AI/Career Tool Directories

ApplyReadyCV is a career tool for private resume and CV self-review. It checks common application issues such as ATS readability, keyword match, achievements, completeness, and remote/freelance/local fit. The current MVP uses browser-based deterministic checks and should not be described as a hiring predictor or guaranteed ATS pass tool. It is best positioned as a free, privacy-first checker for job seekers who want practical editing guidance before applying.

## Safety Rules

- Do not buy directory links.
- Do not use duplicate long descriptions everywhere.
- Do not claim ApplyReadyCV guarantees ATS approval or interviews.
- Do not create fake reviews or ask users for dishonest reviews.
- Do not submit to directories with obvious spam, malware, adult, gambling, or unrelated categories.
- Do not submit to AI-only directories if the only way to fit is making inaccurate AI claims.
