# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-25*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** ApplyReadyCV is a free, privacy-first resume and CV checker for job seekers.

**What it does:** ApplyReadyCV checks resume and CV text for ATS readability, keyword match, achievements, completeness, and application fit. Users can paste text or upload supported files, optionally add a job description, and get practical editing guidance without creating an account or storing resume content.

**Product category:** Free resume checker, CV checker, ATS resume checker, resume keyword checker, resume score checker.

**Product type:** Web application and informational career tool.

**Business model:** Free MVP. No ads, accounts, payments, affiliate links, or paid products are currently included.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Not B2B-focused today. The audience is individual job seekers.

**Decision-makers:** Individual applicants choosing how to improve a resume or CV before applying.

**Primary use case:** A job seeker wants to check whether a resume or CV is readable, complete, tailored to a job description, and appropriate for remote, freelance, or local applications.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Check whether a resume is easy for ATS software and recruiters to read.
- Compare a resume or CV against a real job description for missing keywords.
- Improve application-specific proof for remote work, freelance clients, or local jobs.

**Use cases:**
- ATS resume checker for online applications.
- Resume keyword checker for tailoring to a job description.
- Remote CV checker for async work, tools, ownership, and distributed-team signals.
- Freelance CV checker for service focus, portfolio proof, projects, and client outcomes.
- Local job CV checker for contact details, availability, qualifications, and practical role fit.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Job seekers often submit resumes that are readable to them but hard for software or recruiters to scan, too generic for a specific job, or missing evidence for the application type.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Some checkers overclaim by implying ATS approval or interview guarantees.
- Some tools require accounts, uploads, payments, or unclear data handling before giving value.
- Generic advice often does not adapt to remote, freelance, or local job contexts.
- Keyword tools can encourage stuffing instead of truthful tailoring.

**What it costs them:** Wasted applications, weak first impressions, missed role-specific details, and uncertainty about what to fix next.

**Emotional tension:** Applicants feel anxious, rushed, and unsure whether their CV is being filtered out or simply not communicating fit clearly.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Resume checkers, ATS resume scanners, CV score tools - often fall short when they overpromise, require accounts, or hide privacy details.

**Secondary:** Career coaches, resume writers, job board resume tools - can be useful, but may be paid, slow, or too broad for quick self-checking.

**Indirect:** Manual self-review or generic templates - accessible, but easy to miss keyword, structure, and application-fit gaps.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Privacy-first local browser analysis.
- Free MVP with no account, ads, payments, or database.
- Honest limitations: no guaranteed interviews, job offers, recruiter decisions, or ATS approval.
- Mode-specific feedback for remote, freelance, and local job applications.
- Transparent guidance on ATS readability, keywords, achievements, completeness, and role fit.

**How we do it differently:** ApplyReadyCV keeps resume analysis local in the browser, uses practical deterministic checks, and explains what to review instead of making hiring promises.

**Why that's better:** It gives job seekers fast feedback while preserving trust, privacy, and realistic expectations.

**Why customers choose us:** They want a free, low-friction checker that helps them improve a real application without uploading sensitive resume content to an unknown system.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Can this guarantee ATS approval? | No. It checks common readability, keyword, completeness, and application-fit issues only. |
| Is my resume uploaded or stored? | No. Resume text is analyzed locally in the browser session; there is no account or resume database. |
| Is this as good as a human career coach? | No. It is an editing aid for common issues, not a replacement for professional judgment or employer-specific advice. |
| Should I add every missing keyword? | No. Add only terms that truthfully match your skills, tools, education, projects, or experience. |

**Anti-persona:** Users looking for guaranteed hiring outcomes, fake ATS pass claims, keyword stuffing, fabricated experience, mass outreach spam, or aggressive paid growth shortcuts.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Existing resume advice feels generic, unclear, overhyped, or risky for privacy.

**Pull:** A free, private checker gives specific next edits quickly.

**Habit:** Users may rely on old resume templates, advice from friends, or repeated applications without diagnosis.

**Anxiety:** Users may worry that a checker score is a judgment on their career, that their resume data is stored, or that they will be pushed into paid services.
